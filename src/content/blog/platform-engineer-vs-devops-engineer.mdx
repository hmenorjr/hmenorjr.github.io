## Background

I've been doing business as a "Platform Manager" for almost 2 years now. I never heard this job title before but when I read the description, it looked like task of a "DevOps Engineer".

But then when I tried to look into LinkedIn, there have been posts or opportunities for Platform Engineers. It wasn't there before! So I tried to read through to some of the day to day tasks.

It made me think, what is the difference? So I did some quick research.

## Difference

Platform engineering and DevOps are related but distinct fields within the software development lifecycle. A Platform Engineer typically focuses on building and maintaining internal platforms and tools that support development workflows, aiming to improve the developer experience by providing standardized tools and services.
In contrast, a DevOps Engineer works on integrating development and operations functions to streamline the software delivery process, often involving automation and continuous improvement.

## Job Opportunities
Job opportunities for Platform Engineers often involve creating and managing the internal platforms that developers use, which can include selecting and configuring tools that meet the specific needs of the organization.
These roles may require a strong understanding of cloud technologies, automation, and productivity tools to enhance the efficiency of development teams.

DevOps Engineers, on the other hand, are involved in the broader aspects of software development and operations, including the implementation of agile methodologies, automation of deployment processes, and ensuring that software is delivered efficiently and reliably. They may also be responsible for maintaining the infrastructure that supports the development and deployment of software applications.

## Summary
While both roles contribute to the software development process, Platform Engineers focus on the tools and platforms that support developers. DevOps Engineers focus on the integration and automation of the development and operations processes.
